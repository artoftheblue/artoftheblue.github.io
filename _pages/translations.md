## Correct terminology for translation of LGBTQ+ texts

> **NOTICE:** This page is strictly 18+. By continuing to read further, you confirm that you are of 18 years of age or older.  

> The author does not condone any kind of "propaganda". However, they understand that every human being has an inalienable right to be treated with dignity and respect in accordance with the Russian constitution and to be referred to using correct terminology. 

> Thus, this webpage is a collection of terms to describe LGBTQ+ people from the standpoint of inclusivity and political correctness, created purely out of linguistic interest and not with an intention to promote or impose "non-traditional" values on anyone. This information is further useful in the Russian infospace as it allows to convey information more accurately and preserve the intended meaning.

> This page does not have anything that any Russian person would be able to understand unless they have specifically searched for it and already know English. The collection of Russian words does not make any coherent sentences, does not promote gender transitioning or "non-traditional" sexualities, nor does it have any opinionated descriptions. Everything found below is purely a collection of facts.

## Common Mistakes

| Correct | Incorrect | Comment |
|-:|:-|:-|
| LGBTQ+ | LGBT | Q+ is an umbrella term necessary to include all other sexualities/gender identities |
| LGBTQ+ | LGBTQI+, LGBTQIA+, LGBTQIAAP+, etc. | an overly bloated acronym that looks ridiculous, especially considering that Q+ covers the not previously mentioned in LGBT identities |
| straight, gay | hetero, homo | English has specific words (e. g. straight) to describe this more informally |
| gay, lesbian, bi, trans, queer, etc. | a gay, a lesbian, a bi, a trans, a queer, etc. | those should be adjectives and not nouns, otherwise we treat a person's identity as their entire personality, which is not the case (a homosexual is even more incorrect) |
| homosexual, homosexuality, transgender, etc. | homo-sexual, homo-sexuality, trans-gender, etc. | no hyphens |
| homosexuality, etc. | homosexualism, etc. | the -ism suffix should NOT be used |
| sexuality | sexual orientation | generally acceptable but outdated |  
| transgender identity | transgenderism | same as above
